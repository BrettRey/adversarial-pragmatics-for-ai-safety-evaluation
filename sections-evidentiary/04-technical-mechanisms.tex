\section{Technical Mechanisms}

Technical mechanisms supply pieces of the evidentiary bundle. Structured execution logs record what happened and when. Provenance graphs connect entities, activities, agents, and transformations; W3C PROV gives a general vocabulary for that lineage \parencite{moreau2013provdm}. Content provenance systems bind claims, manifests, hashes, signatures, and timestamps to digital artefacts; C2PA is the most developed current example for media and document provenance \parencite{c2pa2026spec}. Append-only transparency logs make alteration and equivocation more detectable; Certificate Transparency shows how Merkle-tree logs and consistency proofs can support independent auditing \parencite{laurie2013certificate}. Bills of materials expose components and dependencies. Runtime policy enforcement can check explicit constraints before action.

Agentic systems add a language-mediated evidence problem. For tool-using language-model systems, adversarial language can enter through retrieved content and lower-priority instructions, so the evidentiary record also has to preserve instruction source and priority \parencite{greshake2023not,wallace2024instruction,debenedetti2024agentdojo}. Adversarial pragmatics \parencite{reynolds2026adversarialPragmatics} operationalizes that language-status problem as an evaluation target. Authenticated-delegation proposals and agent-audit protocols point toward a stronger record: agent identity, delegated principal, scoped permissions, authorization transitions, audit context, and action records \parencite{south2025authenticated,kuehlewind2026audit}.

The architecture implied by the table is layered. A deployment needs a delegation instrument, a policy-state snapshot, a configuration snapshot, a runtime trace, a preservation mechanism, and a review interface. Weakness at any layer can defeat the later authority claim. A perfect tool-call log doesn't help if the delegation instrument is missing. A signed policy doesn't help if the model treated untrusted retrieved text as a superior instruction. A complete trace doesn't support contestability if the affected reviewer can't access the relevant parts of it.

The same architecture also creates privacy and security risk. Evidence capture should include minimization, redaction, retention schedules, role-based access, and protected review channels. The goal is enough preserved evidence for authorized review without turning every delegated action into an unnecessary archive of sensitive context.
