\section{Conclusion}

The literature disperses evidence across neighbouring fields. Explainability helps with reasons, provenance helps with lineage, audit logging helps with reconstruction, governance law creates documentation duties, and electronic-evidence law supplies concepts for authenticity and integrity. None of these materials alone answers the delegated-authority question, and none makes clean execution evidence a substitute for a warranted decision basis, routed adverse signals, expected controls, or meaningful review.

Evidentiary assurance integrates those materials around one action-level review: whether this specific AI action, by this system, under this delegation, at this time, stayed within authority the institution could validly confer. Delegation assurance supplies the typed authorization claim and transition semantics. Evidentiary assurance asks whether the preserved record makes that claim evaluable and what evidentiary verdict it warrants: supported, defeated, or indeterminate. The proposed test is deliberately defeasible and noncompensatory.

It asks what record would make the authorization claim evaluable, what evidence would support or defeat it, who bears the next burden when a defeater is raised, how sensitive evidence is protected, which people or organizations are candidate answerability bearers, and which forum has the applicable review or remedial powers. Those questions remain subject to forum- and jurisdiction-specific determination. The account is narrower than a finding of full legality, fairness, wisdom, legal liability, or substantive correctness.
