\section{Conclusion}

The literature doesn't ignore evidence; it disperses evidence across neighbouring fields. Explainability helps with reasons, provenance helps with lineage, audit logging helps with reconstruction, governance law creates documentation duties, and electronic-evidence law supplies concepts for authenticity and integrity. None of these materials alone answers the delegated-authority question.

Evidentiary assurance integrates those materials around one action-level claim: this specific AI action, by this system, under this delegation, at this time, stayed within authority the institution could validly confer and can be reconstructed by a reviewer. The proposed sufficiency test is deliberately defeasible. It asks what record would support the authority claim, what predictable challenges could defeat it, who bears the next burden when a defeater is raised, how sensitive evidence is protected, and which human or institutional actor remains answerable when the system acts.
