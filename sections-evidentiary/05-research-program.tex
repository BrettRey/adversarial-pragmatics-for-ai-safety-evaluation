Apparently irrelevant material can't be discarded solely because an investigator later judges it irrelevant. Context without answer evidence may still alter behaviour \parencite{zhang2026illusionRobustness}. That doesn't require publishing or openly retaining every sensitive token. A deployment can bind the complete assembled context through hashes and manifests, keep protected content under tiered retention and access controls, and expose only minimized records by default. Hashes establish identity and tamper evidence, not semantic sufficiency; an authorized reviewer still needs a controlled route to the protected material when the action is contested.

For a program-level claim beyond observed conditions, evidence is version- and target-bound. A projection record should name the source evaluation regime, target deployment regime, claim being projected, suspected changes in model, configuration, context assembly, scaffold, tools, application surface, judge or reviewer state, and time, the target evidence available, and the decision to revalidate, narrow, or demote the claim.

The introduced H.R.~7390 proposal makes version scope concrete by calling for a safety case for each ADS version and tying performance claims to the operational design domain. Its text doesn't define when a system change creates a new version. An evidentiary-assurance implementation would therefore need an identity and change-control rule specifying which changes to software, hardware, vehicle integration, configuration, maps, or ODD specification trigger a new safety case or revalidation, and how a contested event is bound to the applicable version. That is an assurance implication drawn from the proposal, not a requirement the introduced bill already states \parencite{usCongress2026selfDriveAct}.

\term{Projectibility} is the umbrella question of whether an inference beyond the observed result is licensed \parencite{Goodman1955,reynolds2026kindsProjectibilityProfiles}; causal transportability is the stronger case in which a causal query and source and target domains are specified \parencite{pearlBareinboim2014externalValidity}. A change in governing mandate, licensed action, or outcome meaning creates a newly specified target claim rather than an \emph{S}-marker on the old one. Where direct target evaluation is feasible, revalidation is preferable to inference from an annotated graph alone.

This bundle is a research hypothesis, not a finished standard. It needs domain-specific calibration. A consumer chatbot, a medical summarizer, a benefits triage tool, and a procurement assistant don't require the same evidence depth. Their burdens differ by risk, forum, and governing regime.

Each still has to answer the same review questions: who authorized action, whether that authority could validly be delegated, what predicates and transformations supported the decision, what happened, what adverse signals and expected controls were dispositioned, who remains answerable, how sensitive evidence is protected, and how the action can be challenged or corrected. Matched-case testing should determine whether Decision basis detects failures not captured by Authority, Validity, and Scope.

The financial-reporting analogy suggests how such a test could mature. An evidentiary-assurance standard could define control objectives for authorization, decision basis, scope, information flow, persistence, tool use, adverse-signal routing, record integrity, and remediation, then test whether a deployment's controls make action-level review possible.
