\begin{abstract}
AI governance increasingly requires logs, documentation, human oversight, impact assessment, provenance, recourse, and auditability. These requirements create evidence, but they don't yet settle the narrower action-level question: what evidence would support, after the fact, a finding that a specific AI action remained within the authority under which the system was permitted to act? This paper develops an evidentiary-assurance account for delegated authority in AI systems. It distinguishes explanation, provenance, authenticity, integrity, traceability, procedural validity, and delegated scope, then asks how these elements combine into a reviewable evidence bundle for recommendations, decisions, communications, tool calls, transactions, and content-generation acts with legal or organizational effect.
\end{abstract}
