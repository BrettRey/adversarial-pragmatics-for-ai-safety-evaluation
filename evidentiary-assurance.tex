\begin{abstract}
AI governance increasingly requires logs, documentation, human oversight, impact assessment, provenance, recourse, and auditability. These requirements create evidence, but they don't yet settle the narrower action-level question: what does a bounded record warrant about a specific AI action or composite operation under a supplied authorization regime, adjudicating review forum, evidential standard, burden allocation, action class, and declared use? Delegation assurance supplies the normative authorization claim and transition semantics. Evidentiary assurance keeps that claim distinct from four evidence-relative review outputs: what the record warrants about authorization conditions, record adequacy, the identity, duty, and reachability of answerability bearers, and the access, competence, independence, and remedial capacity of object-level forums. It separates support, substantive defeat, record gaps, and unresolved conflict; fixes applicability prospectively; and proposes a versioned evidence bundle and matched-case calibration programme. The result is a defeasible, non-scalar framework for recommendations, decisions, communications, tool calls, transactions, content-generation acts, and bounded execution trajectories with legal or organizational effect.
\end{abstract}
