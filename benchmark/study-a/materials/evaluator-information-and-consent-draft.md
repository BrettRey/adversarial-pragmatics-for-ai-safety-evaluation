# Draft Evaluator Information and Agreement

**Draft only. This is an evaluator information and work-agreement note, not a
human-participant consent form. Do not use it before Brett has settled the
scope, storage, contact, and data-return arrangements.**

## Invitation

You are being invited to provide independent expert evaluation of short
AI-system prompt and response records. The study examines whether stated
criteria for language-mediated control can be applied independently and
consistently. It is not an assessment of you, your employer, or a population of
evaluators.

## Project Status

At present, this is a personal independent project led by Brett Reynolds. It is
not sponsored, hosted, or funded by an institution, and this draft does not
claim ethics approval or institutional affiliation. If that status changes, the
information note will be revised before any work is conducted under the new
arrangement.

## What You Would Do

You would receive a role-specific offline form containing small blind blocks.
Each row presents a prompt and an AI response, with no model identity, author
labels, expected answers, or automated judgments. Depending on your role, you
would rate linguistic/task or policy/safety criteria. You may also provide an
optional short rationale. You would receive only the ZIP for your assigned
role; its opaque package identifier is included in returned files so the
investigator can reject stale or wrong-role submissions.

The intended assignment is all 54 rows for one role, split into three 18-row
blocks that you may complete in separate sittings. The invitation will state a
timed estimate only after the investigator has completed a documented timed
self-pilot of the final interface. You may stop between blocks or decline a row
that you cannot assess; the form includes uncertainty and item-problem options.
Any returned subset of blocks will be marked as partial coverage rather than
represented as a completed assignment.

## Voluntary Evaluator Agreement and Privacy

The evaluation contribution is voluntary. You may decline before beginning or
stop before completing all blocks. No employment, academic, organizational, or
funding relationship depends on contributing an evaluation. This is an unpaid
volunteer contribution: no honorarium, employment benefit, authorship, or other
material consideration is offered or implied.

Please use the assigned pseudonymous rater ID rather than your name in the
form. Downloaded files will be kept locally by the investigator. Public
reporting will use aggregate or sanitized material and will not identify you
without separate written permission.

Each person may serve in only one of the two evaluation roles. A private
assignment record connects your rater ID, assigned role, package version, and
contact identity solely for administration and validation; that mapping is not
part of the research dataset or public release.

Completed block JSON files will be returned to a dedicated Study A email
address. The final instructions will ask you to send only the JSON attachment,
use a subject line containing the assigned rater ID and block ID, and omit real
names, employer or institutional details, private task context, and other
attachments. The study email address is a transfer point, not the long-term
record store: returned files are moved to the local private study directory.

You may request deletion of all returned files associated with your
pseudonymous rater ID until the stated collection-close and analysis-start
cutoff. The final invitation will give that cutoff and a contact route. After
the cutoff, returned files may already have entered de-identified analysis or
aggregate outputs, so deletion cannot be promised.

## Work Conditions and Agreement

Foreseeable burdens are time, interpretive effort, and benign examples involving
instruction conflict or dummy confidentiality boundaries. There may be no direct
personal benefit. Before any live study, this section must name the investigator,
contact route, retention period, scope determination if any, data-return terms,
the collection-close and analysis-start cutoff, and a way to request deletion
before that cutoff. The final form will ask you to confirm that you have read
the information and agree to the evaluation terms.
