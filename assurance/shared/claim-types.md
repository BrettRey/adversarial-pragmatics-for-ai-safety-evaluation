# Shared claim types and projective-claim protocol

This vocabulary keeps the three papers in this repository interoperable without
turning them into chapters of one argument. It is a level-separation device, not
an assurance score or an ontology of every claim an AI system can make.

## Claim types

| Type | Question | Primary bearer | Does not establish |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Normative reference claim | What does the supplied authorization regime license or require? | An action or trajectory under a versioned regime | That the rule is recorded accurately, institutionally operative, or satisfied |
| Recorded representation | What does the available trace, instrument, policy, or evidence bundle encode? | A versioned record | The truth or validity of what it represents |
| Item-level finding | What occurred or was classified in one case? | An item, response, trace, or review | A sample pattern or performance on unobserved cases |
| Sample-level result | What pattern occurred in the observed sample? | A fixed sample under a named configuration | Generalization, transport, or a mechanism |
| Assessment interpretation and use | What may an assessment result be taken to mean or used for? | A configured assessment procedure and proposed use | Validity for a different use, population, or decision |
| Projective claim | What is expected beyond the cases in hand? | A named bearer over a declared unit, population, range, and time/version | Its own warrant or world-side causal ground |
| Causal or mechanistic claim | What intervention changes what result, and through what relation? | A specified causal system and query | Normative authority, legal responsibility, or transport without added assumptions |

## Institutional verdict families

Institutional review should keep at least four verdict families separate:

1. authorization under the supplied regime;
2. record adequacy or reconstructability;
3. identification of a legally or institutionally answerable bearer;
4. contestability, forum, and remedial capacity.

A declared use may require a conjunction of these outputs. The conjunction is a
use rule, not a fact about the ontology: an authorized action may be poorly
recorded, and an unauthorized action may have a pristine record.

## Terminology

- **Assessment validity** concerns the evidential argument for an interpretation
  and use of assessment results. It is not an intrinsic property of a benchmark,
  judge, checklist, or model.
- **Authority validity** or **delegability under R** concerns a legal or
  institutional power supplied by an authorization regime.
- **Record adequacy** concerns whether available evidence permits the stated
  reconstruction or review.
- **Answerability** and **contestability** are distinct from authorization and
  record adequacy.
- A **projective claim** is the inference to unobserved cases. A
  **projectibility profile** is the hypothesized worldly property-and-relation
  pattern that would make that inference non-accidental.
- **Warrant** is evidence and argument licensing the inference. It must not be
  fused with a profile, causal ground, stabilizer, maintainer, or controller.
- A DAG states causal assumptions and queries. It does not by itself establish
  causal attribution, legal responsibility, or normative authority.
- A selection diagram can represent a specified source--target transport
  problem. It cannot transport a changed legal rule, construct meaning, action,
  outcome, or evidential standard.

## Projective-claim protocol

Every nontrivial extension beyond observed cases has four blocks.

### 1. Declaration

Fix the source result, projected outcome, bearer, unit, population, conditions,
allowed and excluded transformations, time/version scope, tolerance, and failure
threshold before target outcomes are inspected.

### 2. Inquiry and use

Fix the intended and excluded uses, evidential standard, treatment of
uncertainty, and minimum useful reach. If a fallback no longer clears the
minimum reach, retire the use.

### 3. Warrant plan

Separate source and target evidence from the assessment-validity argument.
Record content, substantive/response-process, structural, generalizability,
external, and consequential evidence. State world-side profile or ground
hypotheses separately, together with the evidence for each commitment.

Stability and causal order are independent commitments. Neither establishes
maintenance. Corrective control additionally requires a perturbation that
threatens the projected relation, a response coupled to that relation, a change
to lower-scale conditions, and preservation or return of the higher-scale
relation.

### 4. Revision rule

Fix failure triggers, any narrower fallback, the independent-retest requirement,
and the retirement condition prospectively. A scope change made after inspecting
target failures creates a new claim. It does not vindicate the failed claim.

## Version changes

A changed model, scaffold, judge prompt, scoring rule, reviewer population,
authorization regime, legal mandate, action meaning, outcome meaning, or
evidential standard ordinarily creates a new bearer or target claim. A register
may group versions only when the original declaration explicitly included the
variation and the warrant plan tests it.
