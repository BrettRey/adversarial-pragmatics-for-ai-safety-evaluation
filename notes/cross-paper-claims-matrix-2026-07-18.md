# Cross-Paper Claims Matrix: Authority, Causation, Selection, and Evidence

**Date:** 2026-07-21
**Status:** implemented cross-paper vocabulary and ownership gate

## Ownership and boundaries

| Claim or construct | Owner | Supporting role elsewhere | Prohibited inference |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| General projective-claim discipline | *Projectibility: A History and a Diagnostic Framework* | All papers declare the inference, use, warrant, world-side hypothesis, and prospective revision rule | A precise claim is thereby warranted, or a stable result thereby has a known causal ground |
| Target-indexed AI-evaluation application | *AGI Evaluation* | All papers keep evaluation targets and uses explicit | A result projects because its label or score is stable |
| Causal transportability | *AGI Evaluation* generally; Study B for the experiment-specific query | Delegation and Evidentiary name the boundary | An annotated selection diagram establishes transportability |
| Authorization standing and authority transitions | *Delegation Assurance* | AP supplies linguistic contrasts; EA supplies review evidence | A causal edge, message receipt, or latest preference confers standing |
| Trajectory authorization against a declared authorization path | *Delegation Assurance* | AP/Study B tests recognition; EA specifies the evidence bundle | Step-local permission, monitor acceptance, or technical success entails authorization of the bounded trajectory |
| Action-level record adequacy and typed evidentiary verdict vectors | *Evidentiary Assurance* | Delegation supplies the typed authority claim to be assessed | An adequate record entails authorization, or one global verdict may compress authorization, record adequacy, answerability, and contestability |
| Incident-derived evaluation lineage | *Evidentiary Assurance* | AP supplies safe reconstructions; Delegation supplies the claim tested | A selected regression set estimates incident prevalence or proves safeguard efficacy |
| Selective contextual sensitivity | Study B | AP supplies taxonomy and scoring distinctions | Generic response divergence identifies authority sensitivity |
| Horizon-indexed projection | *AGI Evaluation* | Study B registers static-to-live threats; assurance papers name target fields | A static prompt or short trace projects to live tool use or a longer horizon without target-matched evidence |

## Shared typed distinctions

| Term | Working use | Keep separate from |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `projective claim` | licensed inference beyond observed cases, with source, outcome, bearer, unit, population, conditions, transformations, time/version, tolerance, use, warrant, and prospective failure rule | a projectibility profile or causal ground |
| `projectibility profile` | hypothesized worldly property-and-relation pattern that makes a projective claim non-accidentally reliable | the claim's evidential warrant or the declaration itself |
| `assessment validity` | evidential argument supporting an interpretation and use of results from a configured assessment procedure | an intrinsic property of a benchmark, judge, checklist, or model; authority validity |
| `causal transportability` | a specified causal query across declared source and target domains | predictive validity, construct continuity, normative equivalence |
| `selection diagram` | Pearlean diagram marking suspected source--target mechanism or marginal changes | sample-selection DAG; optimizer's curse; benchmark curation |
| `sample selection` | pathway by which a case becomes observed, included, complained about, or reviewed | transportability selection nodes |
| `authority regime R` | declared or adjudicated rules determining valid grants, constraints, and transitions | the deployment's recorded belief about those rules |
| `authorization standing` (`standing` in context) | action- and time-specific capacity under `R` to issue, amend, suspend, revoke, override, release, or review an authority object | public-law locus standi; causal influence; trust level; speaker salience |
| `commitment-protected delegation` | valid mandate whose later unilateral amendment is restricted by declared conditions | emphatic or earlier wording; inherent legitimacy; model preference |
| `answerability bearer` | an individual or organization to which a relevant duty or responsibility can attach over a stated interval | unilateral amendment or override power; the forum that hears a challenge |
| `adjudicating review forum` (`F_review`) | the body or procedure applying the configured evidentiary-assurance protocol under the declared standard and burden | the object-level complaint, appeal, regulatory, judicial, or remedy forum assessed by the record |
| `object-level review/remedy forum` | the body, office, court, regulator, or process whose access, competence, applicable independence, and powers are assessed under `J_C` | `F_review`; the person or organization bearing the underlying duty; a universal right to every listed remedy |
| `record adequacy` | whether the typed record needed to assess an action-level claim is present, interpretable, temporally matched, and sufficiently complete for the stated review | a positive evidentiary verdict on the claim |
| `node-level evidentiary state` | support, substantive defeat, record gap, or conflict for one stated node under regime `R`, adjudicating forum `F_review`, evidential standard `rho`, burden allocation `b`, and available record | applicability (`required`, `optional`, or `not applicable`); record completeness alone; authorization, legality, fairness, or correctness as a whole |
| `typed evidentiary verdict vector` | separate node vectors for authorization evidence (`J_A`), record adequacy (`J_R`), answerability-bearer identity/duty/reachability (`J_B`), and object-forum capacity (`J_C`) | a universal score or ontology-level conjunction; a declared use may prospectively specify which nodes it requires |
| `decision basis` | rule, predicate, source evidence, transformation, temporal fit, uncertainty, and burden supporting a claimed action | authorization, general correctness, legality, or fairness as a whole |
| `adverse-signal routing and disposition` | whether an applicable warning reached a role with a duty and power to respond | proof that every warning defeats every action |
| `normative authorization structure and state` | typed grants, constraints, guarded joint relations, transition competence, and operative state under the declared regime | a recorded propagation graph, execution trajectory, or causal path |
| `bounded execution trajectory` | an ordered set of operations and state changes within declared deployment/session, resource, time, and stopping boundaries | the normative proposition that the sequence was authorized |
| `trajectory authorization` | whether step-local, cumulative, and terminal constraints supplied by the declared regime hold over a bounded trajectory | monitor acceptance, safety, successful completion, or retrospective invention of a missing constraint |
| `recoverability` | whether a declared observer can derive a value from the recorded representation using a declared transformation | contiguous occurrence, actual reconstruction or use, or downstream effect |
| `incident-derived evaluation` | a selected regression or reconstruction set linked to one or more observed incidents | a representative sample or deployment denominator |

## Representation discipline

| Representation | Edge or marker semantics | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Temporal authorization graph | grant, constrain, amend, suspend, revoke, override, release, review | Delegation |
| Bounded execution trajectory | ordered operation, state-change, intervention, pause, operational-continuation, normative-release, and stopping events | Delegation / Evidentiary |
| Provenance graph | used, generated, derived, transformed, preserved | Evidentiary |
| Causal DAG/SCM | hypothesized causal influence and intervention | Study B / AGI |
| Transportability selection diagram | suspected source--target mechanism difference | AGI / Study B |
| Sample-selection DAG | pathway into observation or review | Evidentiary |
| Assurance-case graph | evidence bears on a named node and may support, substantively defeat, expose a record gap, or remain in conflict under the declared procedure | Evidentiary |

No edge vocabulary may migrate across representations without being relabelled and re-argued. In particular, authority is not a causal flow, provenance is not truth, and an `S`-node does not absorb a change in the target claim's normative meaning.

## Source allocation

| Source | Use | Limit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Miller (2005) | multiple principals, limits of the responsive control model, discretion and credible commitment | The three delegation modes are this project's synthesis, not Miller's named taxonomy |
| Robodebt Royal Commission (2023) | predicate/transformation, adverse-signal, access/nonresponse, and institutional-uptake failures | Do not claim that recordkeeping alone would have prevented the scheme |
| Pearl and Bareinboim (2014) | formal meaning and assumptions of transportability selection diagrams | Formalism applies only to specified causal source--target queries |
| Bareinboim, Tian, and Pearl (2014) | distinction between preferential sample selection and transportability | Do not imply that a complaint sketch yields recoverability without a model and data |
| Cobbe, Lee, and Singh (2021) | nearest neighbour for whole-process reviewability and context-sensitive recordkeeping | Novelty must narrow to typed authority, noncompensatory action claims, decision basis, and adverse-signal/control evidence |
| Messick (1995), assessment validity | validity as the integrated evidential argument for interpreting and using configured assessment results | Do not call a benchmark, schema, judge, checklist, or model intrinsically valid; evidence for one use does not validate another |
| Messick (1995), validity of standards | content and performance standards require their own justification and may vary across domains | A common label or node name does not establish cross-module comparability |
| Moynihan, Herd, and Harvey (2015) | access, learning, compliance, and psychological costs as selection pressures on visible objections | Administrative burden does not by itself identify the complaint-selection mechanism in a particular deployment |
| Zalnieriute, Bennett Moses, and Williams (2019) | rule-of-law boundary and Robodebt context | Do not generalize one jurisdiction's doctrines into a universal legal test |
| OpenAI (2026) | first-party cases motivating source-conflict, recoverability, trajectory-monitoring, incident-replay, and horizon-matched evidence fields | Unnamed model; no raw trajectories, denominator, monitor thresholds or error rates, severity rubric, or quantitative replay results; do not infer prevalence, causal safeguard efficacy, or deployment-wide assurance |
