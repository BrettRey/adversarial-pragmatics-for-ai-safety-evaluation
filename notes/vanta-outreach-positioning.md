# Vanta Outreach Positioning

Date: 2026-07-08

## Decision

Vanta is a plausible applied audience for the delegation/evidentiary-assurance work, especially Paper 3. The fit is not that Vanta needs an academic paper. The fit is that Vanta sells into the same institutional problem the paper names: evidence collection, controls, continuous monitoring, audit readiness, AI governance, and proving trust.

## Best framing

Lead with Paper 3, not Paper 1. Paper 3 gives the governance/evidence standard. Paper 2 supplies the AI-security and delegation-assurance frame. Paper 1 supplies a technical evidence layer for instruction conflict, source authority, refusal, and judge validation.

The compact pitch:

> SOX-style controls helped companies prove financial-reporting reliability. Agentic AI needs an analogous evidence standard for delegated machine action. The paper defines the minimum evidence bundle: authority source, scope, model/tool state, runtime trace, oversight record, integrity protection, and accountable institutional actor.

## Why Vanta might care

Vanta already emphasizes automated evidence collection, continuous controls monitoring, SOX ITGC workflows, AI compliance, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, and EU AI Act support. That makes delegation assurance legible as a product/research conversation around what AI-governance evidence has to prove.

The strongest overlap is with controls and evidence, not with generic AI safety. The useful analogy is SOX-style internal control and management responsibility: organizations should not merely assert that an AI action was safe or compliant; they need a reviewable control record around authorization, scope, procedure, integrity, and accountability.

## Boundary conditions

Keep Vanta out of the paper body unless there is a later reason to discuss industry tooling. Vanta is useful for outreach and product positioning, but the scholarly claim should rest on SOX, SEC rules, AI-governance instruments, electronic evidence, provenance standards, and audit-log mechanisms.

Do not imply that responsibility can be delegated to an LLM. A human employee or institutional agent can be held responsible. An LLM can execute delegated action, but responsibility remains with the people and organization that deploy, authorize, constrain, monitor, and rely on it.

## Suggested outreach artifact

Prepare a one-page memo for Vanta or similar GRC vendors:

1. Problem: current AI governance creates records but lacks an action-level evidence standard for delegated machine action.
2. Analogy: SOX-style controls for financial reporting.
3. Proposal: a minimum evidence bundle for AI action.
4. Product relevance: continuous controls monitoring for authority, scope, tool use, information flow, oversight, and record integrity.
5. Research assets: Paper 3 as lead, Paper 2 as security frame, Paper 1 as evaluation layer.
