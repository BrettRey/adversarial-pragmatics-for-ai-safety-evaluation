## Proposed one-page attachment
### Study A: expert evaluation of fixed AI prompt-response objects
**Purpose and materials.** Study A is a methodological component of _Adversarial Pragmatics for AI Safety Evaluation_. Its complete object set is already fixed: 18 benign prompts, each answered by three local AI models, for 54 non-human prompt-response objects. The study asks for which of those objects a prespecified evaluation procedure yields a criterion-specific supported label and how those object labels compare with a frozen author snapshot and two automated comparators. It is not a model leaderboard and does not make population claims about prompts, models, or people.

**Expert roles and procedure.** Six qualified expert evaluators are planned: three in a linguistic/task role and three in a policy/safety role. The role pools are person-disjoint, and anyone who materially developed the objects is excluded from first-pass evaluation. Evaluators receive blind, role-specific offline forms and a prescriptive codebook with defined criteria and permitted labels. Each person applies one codebook to all 54 objects in three 18-object blocks. The current administrative workload estimate is **30–40 minutes per block**. The offline forms record coarse block start, completion, elapsed, and save timestamps for local saving and return-integrity checks; those fields are validated administratively, remain private, and are not copied into Study A analysis tables. Timing is not a study outcome.

The linguistic/task codebook covers task success and specified pragmatic features. The policy/safety codebook covers compliance with visible rules, refusal calibration, and visible information-flow or security-boundary features. Evaluator returns contain criterion labels, criterion-scoped confidence, and optional brief rationales. These are used as assessments of the prompt-response objects under the stated criteria. Confidence and rationale fields remain private and are not analysis or release variables; they may be consulted only after a named object--criterion cell or rubric question has been flagged for adjudication.

**Unit of analysis and estimands.** Every planned estimand is indexed to a prompt-response object, object-criterion cell, fixed paired contrast, or object-label comparison. The primary summaries are the numbers and fractions of the 54 objects for which the role-specific procedure yields a unanimous- or majority-supported substantive label, plus comparisons of those labels with the author and automated labels. Contested cells and unresolved objects remain visible. Agreement supplies or withholds support for an object label and helps diagnose the operation of the measurement procedure; it is not treated as correctness or as an outcome about the people producing the ratings.

Study A has no evaluator-level estimand. It will not collect or analyze demographics; infer to a population of evaluators; compare professional, institutional, theoretical, or demographic groups; or report evaluator severity, drift, individual performance, confidence calibration, reasoning style, or person-level timing. Names/contact details and qualification, professional-network, relationship, and conflict-of-interest information are held separately for selection and administration. Each expert's repeated ratings are linked by a study ID for return validation and object-level aggregation. These records are not research outcomes or analysis variables. Public reporting is aggregate or object-level and will not identify an evaluator without separate permission.

**Terms for evaluators.** Before beginning, each expert receives a plain-language evaluator information and voluntary agreement note describing the task, workload, linked study ID, separate administrative records, data handling, attribution/confidentiality choices, withdrawal terms, and contact route. Recruitment is purposive through professional networks. Any supervisory, collegial, student, employment, or other dependency relationship with the investigator will be declared and addressed before invitation. An expert may decline or stop without penalty. The planned contribution is unpaid and offers no employment or academic benefit, authorship, or other material consideration. The examples are benign; the foreseeable burdens are time and interpretive effort.

Study A will be conducted off duty, without Humber funding, resources, systems, facilities, recruitment access, sponsorship, hosting, or sanction. It will not recruit from the Humber community or collect Humber institutional data. No recruitment or collection has begun.

## Internal source and justification notes — do not send as part of the attachment
- [TCPS 2 (2022), Article 2.1 and its Application](https://ethics.gc.ca/eng/tcps2-eptc2_2022_chapter2-chapitre2.html) uses a broad participant definition: people whose data or responses are relevant to the research questions. The same Application expressly distinguishes interaction used to obtain information from people who are not themselves the focus of the research, giving authorized personnel providing role-based information as its example. It directs researchers to seek the REB's opinion when applicability is uncertain. Study A sits at that boundary: human ratings are relevant inputs, but the prespecified estimands concern the evaluated objects and procedure, not the people.
  
- [PRE Scope interpretation #12](https://ethics.gc.ca/eng/policy-politique_interpretations_scope-portee.html) makes the focus-of-inquiry distinction explicit: ordinary-course information can be the research focus rather than the staff member's views, whereas personal opinions outside the person's role require review. The staff example is not identical to independent expert scoring, so it supports the question presented to Humber rather than establishing an exemption.
  
- [PRE Scope interpretation #19](https://ethics.gc.ca/eng/policy-politique_interpretations_scope-portee.html) says that human contributors who collect data about non-human objects may be research partners without necessarily being participants, while contributors whose own data or responses are studied can be participants. This is a useful role analogy, not a direct ruling on Study A.
  
- [Humber REB mandate](https://humber.ca/ppdr/services/research/humbers-research-ethics-board.html) covers research involving human participants within Humber's jurisdiction or under its auspices, including work using Humber resources or sanctioned by Humber and conducted by Humber faculty, staff, or students. The page also says approval is required before covered research begins.
  
- Humber's [Ethical Conduct for Research Involving Humans procedure (AC 200 P1)](https://humber.ca/legal-and-risk-management/assets/files/pdfs/procedures/Ethical%20Conduct%20for%20Research%20Involving%20Humans%20AC200P1.pdf) adopts the broad TCPS participant definition, assigns final review decisions to the HREB, and provides both full and delegated review routes. Read with the public mandate, it does not make the status of an off-duty, unfunded employee project with no Humber resources or sanction sufficiently clear to self-determine; that is why the draft asks for a written scope decision.
  
- Humber's [current HREB deadlines](https://humber.ca/ppdr/services/research/humbers-research-ethics-board/reb-deadlines.html) say the Board does not operate from June 19 through September 1, 2026; applications submitted after the June 8 delegated-review deadline will not be reviewed until activities resume in September. This affects scheduling, not the substantive scope question.
  
- Brett Reynolds, [_Expert Grammaticality Judges as Evaluators, Not Participants: Grammaticality Judgments, Rater Roles, and Research Ethics Review_](../../../retarget/expert-grammaticality-judges-evaluators/evaluators_v_participants_reynolds.pdf) (2026), supplies the local role test used to shape Study A: an evaluator applies a stated, challengeable criterion to a target object; qualification, independence, blinding, and adjudication are measurement safeguards. The paper also marks the limiting case: if the design estimates distributions or traits of the judges, they are participants for that analysis. It expressly leaves institutional scope decisions to local boards, so it is a design justification, not authority for exemption.
