# Commitment, Causation, Selection, and Evidence: Cross-Paper Integration Plan
**Date:** 2026-07-18
**Status:** implemented and verified; Study B production remains gated and AGI alignment remains coordinated externally
**Scope:** _Adversarial Pragmatics_, Study B, _Delegation Assurance_, _Evidentiary Assurance_, and a coordinated alignment audit of _AGI Evaluation_
**Hard boundary:** no change to Study A items, protocol, estimands, analysis, or package
## Decision
Apply the recent source work through a division of labour rather than by putting one master graph into every paper.

- _AGI Evaluation_ owns the general account of target-indexed projectibility and the Pearlean selection-diagram formalism. Its live revision already has the needed diagram and already denies that a diagram by itself establishes transportability.

- _Delegation Assurance_ owns the normative and temporal authorization model: who has standing to issue, amend, suspend, revoke, override, release, or review a mandate, and when. Credible commitment becomes one mode of delegation, not a general defence of insulation.

- _Evidentiary Assurance_ owns the claim--evidence structure: decision-basis lineage, adverse-signal routing, expected controls, review powers, and the distinction among authorization, provenance, causal, transport, and sample-selection graphs.

- _Adversarial Pragmatics_ states the authorization--causation--projection distinction compactly and keeps the flagship empirical paper focused. It does not become a causal-inference treatise.

- Study B operationalizes the distinctions through controlled interventions, typed reference records, a causal evaluation DAG, and a source--target projection register.


This is projectibility-first throughout. Causal transportability is one stronger, query-specific form of projectibility; it is not the whole framework. No paper will revive HPC, a maintenance-mechanism requirement, a universal robustness score, or a single delegation-assurance score.
## What the source work changed
The Miller and Robodebt originals do more than confirm the current manuscripts.

1. Miller makes the ordinary responsive principal--agent model too narrow. Delegation can involve multiple or competing principals, protected expert discretion, or a credible commitment whose point is to limit a grantor's later unilateral control. Divergence from the latest expressed preference can therefore be mandate fidelity rather than goal drift.

2. Robodebt shows that a reconstructable workflow can still rest on an unsupported decision predicate. Annual aggregate income was made to stand in for fortnightly income; contrary legal and review signals existed but were not given effective circulation or consequence; and access design helped select which objections became visible. More logging alone would not repair those failures.

3. Pearl helps only after the question is typed. A causal DAG represents hypothesized influence; a transportability selection diagram marks suspected source--target mechanism differences. Neither represents normative standing. If the target authority regime changes what action is licensed, the reference proposition or estimand changes; that is not merely an `S`-node on the same query.

4. A formal influence diagram could model why institutional actors surrender future discretion, but it would be decorative in the present papers without specified actors, decisions, utilities, and strategically discriminating predictions. Defer it.

## Tested assumptions and falsification conditions
| Working assumption | What would make it false | Required response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Temporal, action-specific standing adds explanatory value beyond static scope. | It changes no failure classification, evidence field, or controlled contrast. | Remove the formal extension rather than retain ceremonial notation. |
| Commitment-protected delegation is a distinct and testable mode. | Competent adjudicators cannot distinguish an operative protection or release condition from emphatic wording in benign cases. | Keep it as a conceptual limitation only; do not create a Study B family. |
| `Decision basis` is a distinct evidentiary primitive. | Existing Authority, Validity, and Scope fields reliably expose a cleanly authorized but predicate-invalid workflow on matched cases. | Fold it into those primitives and retain only the transformation checks. |
| Adverse-signal routing can be evaluated without claiming that every warning defeats every action. | A warning cannot be linked by time, applicability, recipient, duty, and possible consequence to the contested claim. | Do not call it a defeater; record it as background information. |
| A Pearlean selection diagram earns its place in Study B. | There is no specified source domain, target domain, causal query, target data, or suspected changed mechanism. | Use a plain projection-threat register and direct revalidation instead. |
| The licensed action can be specified independently of observed model behaviour. | Experts still cannot agree after the fictional authorization rules are clarified. | Revise or remove the item; a DAG cannot manufacture a reference answer. |
| A standing contrast isolates standing rather than wording, recency, or load. | Matched-placebo effects rival the control contrast, or role/order reversals erase it. | Redesign the family and withdraw the authority-sensitivity interpretation. |
| Cross-paper coherence requires shared distinctions, not duplicate figures. | A reader must reconstruct an essential argument from another unpublished manuscript. | Add the minimum self-contained explanation locally, but do not duplicate the full formalism. |
| Source and target preserve the same causal and normative question. | The target regime changes the licensed action or the meaning of the outcome. | Declare a new target claim or estimand; do not describe this as ordinary transport. |
## Shared representation and terminology rules
Every revision will follow the same graphical grammar.

| Representation | Edge or marker meaning | What it can support | What it cannot establish |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Temporal authorization graph | grants, constrains, amends, suspends, revokes, overrides, releases, reviews | standing, scope, and valid state transitions under a declared regime | causal uptake, truth, or legal validity beyond the stipulated/adjudicated regime |
| Provenance graph | used, generated, derived, transformed, preserved | lineage and reconstruction | causation, authorization, or substantive correctness |
| Causal DAG/SCM | hypothesized causal influence and interventions | causal attribution under stated assumptions | standing, permission, or normative validity |
| Transportability selection diagram | suspected source--target mechanism or marginal differences | analysis of a specified causal query across domains | construct equivalence, normative equivalence, or transportability by inspection |
| Sample-selection DAG | pathways into observation, inclusion, complaint, or review | diagnosis of observed-case bias | population incidence without assumptions and denominator data |
| Assurance-case graph | supports, warrants, rebuts, undercuts | an explicit noncompensatory claim--evidence structure | the truth or sufficiency of evidence merely because it is linked |

Terminological rules:

- `Projectibility` remains the umbrella for licensed inference beyond the observed result.

- Reserve `causal transportability` for a declared causal query with source and target domains.

- Keep Pearlean `selection diagram`, sample selection, and optimization/curation selection distinct.

- Use `commitment-protected delegation` analytically; `binding to the mast` or `Ulysses` may appear only as an explanatory gloss.

- Keep causal responsibility, legal liability, institutional answerability, review power, and override authority separate.

- The ultimately answerable people or human-governed organizations need not possess every amendment or override power in the delegation chain.

- A later message can causally alter behaviour without validly altering the authorization state.

## Paper-by-paper implementation
### 1. Delegation Assurance: own the authority model
This is the first substantive revision because its vocabulary becomes Study B's reference schema.

1. Add a compact `Delegation beyond responsiveness` treatment grounded in Miller:

  - responsive delegation;

  - protected expert discretion; and

  - commitment-protected delegation.


  Treat these as this paper's analytic distinctions, not Miller's named taxonomy. General claims should allow plural, collective, or competing grant sources.

2. Make standing temporal and operation-specific. For an authority object `q`, role or source `x`, time `t`, and operation

  `o in {issue, direct, amend, suspend, revoke, override, release, review}`,

  define `Stand_R(x, o, q, t)`. A purported transition changes `A_R(t)` only when the actor has the relevant standing and the regime's guard or release conditions are satisfied. State explicitly that this is a normative update rule, not a causal equation.

3. Extend the assurance record and delegation matrix with:

  - governing source or sources;

  - operation-specific standing;

  - effective interval;

  - amendment, revocation, and override structure;

  - joint approvals or guarded/hyperedge conditions;

  - release, expiry, emergency, and review conditions; and

  - the recorded result of each purported transition.

4. Repair failure categories:

  - `goal drift` requires divergence from the operative mandate, not merely from the latest preference expressed by a principal;

  - add authorized resistance to an invalid override;

  - add improper principal/intermediary override or interference; and

  - keep answerability distinct from unilateral control.

5. Add one controlled temporal-authority contrast to the comparative programme: hold the later directive, issuer, action, and causal exposure fixed; vary whether the earlier mandate is ordinarily revocable, validly protected, or validly released. Include a surface-matched but institutionally inert protection statement.

6. Keep the graph semantics honest. Rename existing TikZ `causal` styles that merely show handoff or content flow. Use solid `flow` edges and separately typed authority relations. Do not call the authorization representation a DAG: joint conditions, cycles, and guarded transitions may be required.

7. Figure budget: retain at most the current number of full figures. Rework one existing two-panel figure to make the revocable/protected temporal contrast visible, or compress the contrast into the existing typed-graph treatment. Do not add a Pearlean selection diagram or an influence diagram here.

8. Clarify the projection paragraph: current `T_pred` and `T_rec` targets are predictive and reconstructive. A changed authority regime can change the reference proposition; formal causal transport requires an additional causal query and model.


**Delegation gate:** commitment protection is not automatically legitimate or desirable. It may entrench harm or frustrate lawful democratic control. Every protected mandate therefore needs declared validity, review, and release conditions.
### 2. Evidentiary Assurance: own the evidence model
1. Add `Decision basis` as a provisional tenth primitive, after Scope. Its record includes:

  - governing rule and version;

  - required factual and legal predicates;

  - source evidence and data definitions;

  - transformations, aggregation, imputation, and temporal granularity;

  - uncertainty and burden allocation; and

  - the mapping from the rule used to the claimed source of authority.


  It does not establish authorization, general substantive correctness, legality, or fairness by itself.

2. Extend Procedure and Oversight with `adverse-signal routing and disposition` rather than creating another primitive immediately. Record warnings, legal advice, adverse review decisions, complaints, incidents, recipient roles, deadlines, dispositions, escalations, and downstream changes.

3. Add an `expected-control record`: triggering condition, required act, responsible role, due time, completion or disposition, and monitoring coverage. Absence of a completion record counts against a claim only when expected logging completeness is itself supported.

4. Strengthen answerability and review. A meaningful review role must be reachable, competent, empowered, sufficiently independent where required, able to access the record, and under a relevant duty to review, correct, halt, or remediate. Identification alone is insufficient.

5. Rewrite the Robodebt case around three non-interchangeable failures:

  - predicate/transformation failure: annual totals did not establish actual fortnightly income;

  - adverse-signal failure: contrary legal advice, tribunal decisions, complaints, and warnings lacked effective escalation or consequence; and

  - observation-selection failure: channel design made response harder while nonresponse helped trigger averaging.


  The conclusion is not that the scheme merely lacked records. It lacked warranted decision-basis lineage, required-control evidence, and warning routes attached to powers and duties.

6. Replace the linear `evidence chain` figure with converging, noncompensatory evidence rails. Authorization, decision basis, execution/provenance, adverse-signal/control records, preservation/integrity, and contestability/answerability converge on a reviewable authority claim. This prevents the figure from implying that oversight is only downstream of execution.

7. Add the shared representation table above in compact form. The paper should own the assurance-case/evidence graph; it should not force authorization, provenance, and causal relations into one DAG.

8. Add one small sample-selection sketch only if it changes the audit rule:

  `action defect / awareness + channel access and capacity + recording/routing practice -> observed complaint -> routed review`.

  Its operational conclusion is that complaint counts are fire alarms, not prevalence estimates. `No complaints` requires exposure, access, delivery, and opportunity denominators. Label this a sample-selection DAG, not a transportability selection diagram.

9. Add a projection record to the proposed evidence bundle: source evaluation regime, target deployment regime, suspected changed mechanisms, affected claims, target evidence, and the revalidation decision. A changed legal mandate or outcome meaning requires a newly specified target claim rather than an `S`-node.


**Evidentiary gate:** after matched-case testing, retain `Decision basis` as a separate primitive only if it detects failures not exposed by Authority, Validity, and Scope.
### 3. Adversarial Pragmatics: make the boundary explicit and stay empirical
Keep these edits surgical and page-neutral where possible.

1. State three questions near the evaluation bottleneck:

  - authorization: which context is entitled to control the action;

  - causation: which context actually changed the response; and

  - projection/transport: where the measured relation is licensed to recur.


  Causal sensitivity does not establish standing; standing does not guarantee causal uptake.

2. Make source priority modestly temporal and action-specific in the taxonomy: amendment, revocation, expiry, joint approval, and protected-discretion cases are possible.

3. Repair transcript diagnosis by distinguishing unauthorized agent drift, authorized resistance to an invalid override, and improper principal/intermediary interference.

4. State that future controlled items obtain their licensed action from an independently specified authorization record. The intervention tests whether behaviour responds to that record; the behaviour does not determine validity.

5. Repair the study chronology so readers see three layers: completed historical pilot, gated fixed-object Study A re-adjudication, and separate design-only Study B causal contrasts. Do not retrofit a causal estimand, DAG, or transport claim into Study A.

6. Add one cross-paper boundary sentence in the implications: this paper tests contextual uptake under interventions; _Delegation Assurance_ supplies the typed authority semantics; _Evidentiary Assurance_ states what record supports the resulting claim; _AGI Evaluation_ supplies the general projection discipline.


**Page budget:** no more than approximately half a net page. Prefer replacement and compression over a new conceptual section or figure.
### 4. Study B: make the distinctions operational
Add a `Representation discipline` section and three aligned artifacts.

1. **Typed authorization/reference record.** Per item, record actor or role, source span, licensed action, scope, effective interval, and standing to issue, direct, amend, suspend, revoke, override, release, and review. This record supplies the expected action `Y*`; it is not represented as though normativity causally produces the model response.

2. **Causal evaluation DAG.** At minimum:

  ```text
  assigned condition -> realized assembled context -> response/action -> observed score
                             ^                         ^                 ^
                    scaffold/truncation          tool surface     judge/info state
  ```

  Random assignment licenses within-configuration intervention contrasts. It does not establish an internal reasoning mechanism.

3. **Projection register / selection diagram.** For each projected causal inference, declare:

  - source and target domains;

  - the causal query;

  - target data;

  - suspected changes in context assembly/truncation, model/configuration, scaffold/tool realization, application surface, judge/rubric/information state, and provider version or time; and

  - the rule for direct revalidation, narrowing, or demotion.


  If these requirements are absent, call the artifact a `selection-node threat map`, not a formal transportability result. Prefer direct revalidation whenever target access makes it feasible.

4. Reserve roughly four of the planned 24 bases for commitment-protected authority. The canonical four conditions are:

  - `C0`: a later instruction validly amends an ordinarily revocable mandate;

  - `C1`: the earlier instrument removed unilateral amendment standing, so the same later instruction must be resisted;

  - `N0`: comparable inert context load; and

  - `N1`: surface-matched protection language appears in quotation, example, or another non-operative context.


  Add action-specific controls so the source remains authoritative for unrelated operations. Otherwise the task degenerates into `follow earlier text`.

5. Extend the item schema and validator with temporal standing and transition fields. Build four benign base scenarios across ordinary revocation, joint amendment, expiry/release, and operative-versus-ceremonial language.

6. Make construction selection explicit:

  `private candidate -> abstract-card eligibility -> reconstructability/privacy pass -> pilot survival -> production inclusion`.

  Included items support claims about the constructed domain, not prevalence in private histories or deployment populations.

7. Protect the production claim from pilot adaptation. Any item selected or rewritten after model-output inspection remains development-only. Production inference requires untouched held-out items or a fresh frozen confirmatory set; the existing split-sample tail rule does not by itself solve construction selection.

8. Add `benchmark/study-b/projection-register.md`, the revised schema, a validator, and explicit demotion rules. Do not add do-calculus, transport formulae, or a single selective-sensitivity score unless a concrete query and decision use make them necessary.


**Study B gate:** no external annotation or claim-bearing collection until the separate institutional-scope decision, source/target declarations, held-out construction rule, schema, codebook, model/configuration list, and analysis contrasts are frozen.
