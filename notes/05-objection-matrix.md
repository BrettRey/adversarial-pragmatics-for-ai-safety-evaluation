# Objection Matrix

| Objection | Risk | Planned response |
|-----------|------|------------------|
| This is just prompt injection under another name. | Reviewers see no contribution beyond existing security work. | Show that prompt injection is one family inside a broader instruction-source, authority, quotation, scope, and transcript-interpretation taxonomy. |
| The taxonomy is too linguistic for safety evaluators. | The paper reads as disciplinary export rather than usable method. | Keep every category tied to item construction, annotation, and scoring. |
| The benchmark is too small. | Empirical result looks anecdotal. | Present the first paper as a controlled development pass with transparent construction principles and a scaling plan. |
| Gold labels are arbitrary because policies are ambiguous. | The benchmark seems invalid. | Treat policy ambiguity as a measured outcome and separate task success, policy compliance, risk, confidence, and failure attribution. |
| Harmless payloads reduce ecological validity. | Prompt-injection/safety people may want realistic harms. | Use harmless payloads for structural validation first; add realistic policy-safe items only after source and safety review. |
| LLM judges are not reliable enough. | Autograder results may not be credible. | Make judge failure part of the research question and compare with adjudicated expert labels and rule-aided checks. |
| This is not ML enough. | Safety labs may want model-internal work. | Position as eval-design, gold-set, policy-taxonomy, red-team interpretation, and safety-documentation work. |
