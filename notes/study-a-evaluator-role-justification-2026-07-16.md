# Study A evaluator-role justification

**Decision date:** 2026-07-16
**Status:** design justification; not an institutional scope determination
**Collection status:** closed pending written Humber determination

## Decision

Study A is designed methodologically as expert evaluation of a fixed set of 54
non-human prompt--response objects. The experts serve inside a prespecified
measurement procedure; they are not the target population or unit of the
planned estimands.

This classification does **not** follow merely because the contributors are
experts, because the files use study IDs, or because the project avoids
demographic questions. It follows from what the design asks, what the codebook
requires the experts to do, and what the analysis is permitted to claim.

## Design-to-role justification

| Design feature | Why it supports an evaluator role |
|---|---|
| Fixed 54-row object set | The measured targets are already-existing AI prompt--response objects; no population inference to people is planned. |
| Prescriptive role-specific codebooks | Experts apply stated, challengeable criteria and fixed label vocabularies rather than report unconstrained personal reactions. |
| Qualification, independence, blinding, and item-developer exclusion | These are measurement-quality and conflict-of-interest controls, not respondent-sampling variables. |
| Agreement used to support or withhold an object label | Convergence qualifies an object--criterion measurement result; it does not estimate a trait or distribution of evaluators. |
| Author--panel and automated--panel comparisons | These compare object-labelling procedures over the fixed set. |
| Stable rater IDs and person-disjoint role pools | IDs support return integrity, repeated-rating linkage, and criterion separation. Pseudonymity is not the basis of the role classification. |

## Boundary conditions built into the design

The evaluator classification remains defensible only while all of these
guardrails hold:

1. Every planned estimand is indexed to a prompt--response object,
   object--criterion cell, paired contrast, or object-label comparison.
2. Study A does not estimate or report demographics, rater severity or drift,
   individual performance profiles, subgroup differences, confidence
   calibration, reasoning styles, person-level timing or burden, or any
   population quantity over evaluators.
3. Confidence and optional rationales are limited to item-level uncertainty,
   interpretation, and adjudication metadata. They are not standalone outcomes
   or material for profiling people.
4. Contested ratings and task/policy combinations are reported as properties of
   object--criterion cells or the specified rubric/procedure. Because criterion
   and person pool vary together, they cannot identify differences between
   linguistic and policy evaluator populations.
5. Network breadth and role expertise are qualification, independence, and
   conflict-of-interest controls, not a claim of representative sampling.
6. The disclosed workload is a conservative administrative estimate of 30--40
   minutes per 18-row block. Interface timestamps are excluded from research
   estimands and public result claims.
7. Names, contact details, qualification/COI information, and relevant
   supervisory, collegial, student, employment, or dependency relationships are
   separate administrative records used for eligibility, voluntariness, and
   return integrity. They are not analysis variables or reportable outcomes.

If a later analysis makes the evaluator's characteristics, reactions, timing,
confidence calibration, reasoning, or group membership an outcome, the role
must be reassessed for that analysis before it is added.

## Institutional question is separate

The methodological role test is evidence to present to Humber, not authority to
self-declare an exemption. TCPS 2 uses a broad response-based definition while
also distinguishing people who supply information about an object from people
who are themselves the research focus. The Panel on Research Ethics likewise
recognizes, in its citizen-science interpretation, that people may gather data
about non-human objects without necessarily becoming participants. Those are
relevant analogies, not a binding decision on this project.

Humber's public materials do not resolve how this unfunded, employee-led
project and its proposed expert-evaluator role will be classified. Study A
therefore requires a written HREB response before recruitment, distribution,
or collection. If Humber requires REB review
or participant consent, the project will follow that route and revise the
materials. If Humber determines the activity is outside review, the written
determination will be retained in the private evidence record.

## Operational consequences

- The collection gate requires separate hash-bound copies of the sent scope
  request and Humber's written response, binds the request to the current
  analysis plan and protocol through an explicit manual confirmation, and
  requires manual review of response provenance, meaning, and conditions. A
  self-attested project-posture statement or file hash alone is not enough.
- The annotated freeze tag must be created only after the written response has
  been received and the determination recorded, so the frozen design can
  reflect any conditions.
- The return route is whichever access-controlled channel the final scope/data
  plan permits. A dedicated mailbox and synthetic email-transfer test are not
  scientific requirements.
- The private assignment attestation proves the registry bytes have not
  changed and that its internal identifiers are coherent. Only the
  investigator's roster check can establish the real-world identity and
  person-disjointness of the assigned experts.
- Recruitment through professional networks does not erase possible power or
  dependency relationships. Those relationships must be declared and addressed
  before invitation even though they are not research variables.
- The evaluator information/agreement note covers voluntary contribution,
  workload, data handling, attribution/confidentiality, withdrawal, retention,
  and recognition. It becomes a participant-consent instrument only if Humber
  requires that path.
