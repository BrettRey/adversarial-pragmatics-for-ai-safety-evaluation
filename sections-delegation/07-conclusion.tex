\section{Conclusion}

Delegation assurance names an evidence standard for the claim that a machine action remained within delegated authority. That standard matters because AI systems increasingly act through language, tools, memory, and evaluators whose boundaries are easy to collapse.

The framework maps familiar AI-security failures to failures of authority uptake, action licensing, information flow, persistence, audit reconstruction, or evaluation. It stakes its usefulness on the comparative test of Section~\ref{sec:comparative-test}, which asks authority status to predict or reconstruct what capabilities, per-call authorization, and causal attribution don't. The mapping stays bounded: it applies when the system's action rights, limits, or review record are at issue, without turning every model error into a delegation failure.

Its practical test is whether evaluators and deployers record enough for a third party to reconstruct who licensed the action, what limits applied, how language-mediated authority was interpreted, which tool gates were crossed, and which accountable actor remains answerable.
